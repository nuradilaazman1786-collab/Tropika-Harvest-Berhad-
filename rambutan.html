Historical Background

Rambutan is natively indigenous to the humid and tropical lowlands of the Malaysian-Indonesian archipelago. For centuries, rambutan trees have been a cherished centerpiece of traditional village house compounds that represent a welcoming local hospitality as families gathered under their heavy red clusters during mid-summer harvest peaks.

Botanical Characteristics

This medium size evergreen tree produces striking oval fruits arranged in heavy and dangling clusters which belong to the Sapindaceae family as Nephelium lappaceum. Its outer rind is lined with soft and flexible structures called spinterns just like spine. Peeling the rind reveals an oval as well as translucent white aril that is firm, crisp and cleanly detaches from the seed in premium clones.

Health Benefits

  • Immune Defense: Abundant in natural Vitamin C which accelerates iron absorption and strengthens daily immunity against common infections.
  • Hydration and Vitality: Composed of high water content and organic fructose that provide a refreshing way to stay hydrated and energized.
  • Skin and Tissue Support: Rich in copper and manganese minerals that assist body cells in cellular synthesis and maintaining healthy skin texture.
💡 Did You Know? The name "Rambutan" is derived directly from the Malay word "rambut" which translates to "hair"! This perfectly describes the soft with external spines that cover the entire surface with hair of this exotic fruit.
