Historical Background

Mata Kucing is a native close relative of the regional longan fruit, historically gathered wild across old-growth Malaysian rainforest sectors. It earned its descriptive name meaning "Cat's Eye" in Malay because its transluscent aril exposes a prominent dark seed underneath which mimick a feline eye when sliced open.

Botanical Characteristics

Belong to the Sapindaceae family as Dimocarpus longan, this massive tropical tree yields clusters of small and globose fruits wrapped in a rough together with brownish leathery pericarp. Compared to imported longans, the true local variety features a slightly thinner flesh layer but commands a much muskier as well as concentrated honey flavor compound.

Health Benefits

  • Energy Boosting: High levels of natural sucrose and glucose provide a clean along with instant energy lift.
  • Calming Effects: Traditionally relied upon in traditional herbal preparations to reduce fatigue and promote rest.
  • Mineral Abundance: Contains healthy amounts of potassium and iron that support balanced blood circulation.
💡 Did You Know? True wild Malaysian Mata Kucing varieties have a textured and bumpy shell that sets them apart from smooth longans that offer a richer flavor profile prized by fruit specialists!
