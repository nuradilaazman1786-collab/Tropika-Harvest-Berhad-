Historical Background

Malaysia's tropical and humid climate all year round is perfect for guava trees, they continue to thrive and are now considered a local favorite. It adapted rapidly to the equatorial climate of the Malaysian peninsula which earn the local name "Jambu Batu" also known as Stone Rose Apple due to its firm and unyielding crunchy texture compared to native watery rose apples.

Botanical Characteristics

Belong to the Myrtaceae family, Psidium guajava grows on small and smooth-barked evergreen trees. The fruit is oval or round with a rough plus light green skin that turns yellowish when soft. The interior white or pale pink flesh is highly crisp which surrounded a central core packed with small and hard edible seeds or developed as a fleshy seedless matrix in modern cultivars.

Health Benefits

  • Ultimate Vitamin C Source: Contains up to four times more natural Vitamin C than standard citrus fruits, significantly boosting immune defenses.
  • Blood Sugar Regulation: Features a remarkably low glycemic index and high dietary fiber content that helps maintain balanced blood glucose.
  • Cardiovascular Health: High levels of soluble fiber and potassium aid in improving lipid profiles and supporting healthy heart functions.
💡 Did You Know? Jambu Batu is one of the few tropical fruits that is frequently enjoyed and commercially harvested while still technically underripe! The local preference leans heavily toward the firm and sound crunch of the green fruit which often paired with a dash of local sour plum powder.
