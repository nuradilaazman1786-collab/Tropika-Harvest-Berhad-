Historical Background

Duku is a fruit heavily synonymous with the traditional agricultural landscape of the East Coast of Peninsular Malaysia. Historically, the most celebrated duku varieties such as the Duku Terengganu, have been cultivated across generations along river basins that transform it into a signature seasonal delicacy whenever local harvest cycles peak.

Botanical Characteristics

Classified under the Meliaceae family as Lansium domesticum, duku fruits develop in dense clusters on sturdy canopy trees. Unlike its close relative, langsat which features a thin and a rind that rich in latex, the duku possesses a notably thicker skin that becomes completely free from sap upon ripening to develop a firm, sweet and translucent white flesh segments.

Health Benefits

  • Vitamin B Complex: Rich in Riboflavin (B2) and Thiamine (B1) which optimize metabolic energy processing and support vital nervous system functions.
  • Natural Antioxidants: Packed with active polyphenolic compounds that counter harmful free radicals and protect cellular health.
  • High Fiber Content: Enhances gastrointestinal motility naturally while supporting long-term digestive and gut flora health.
💡 Did You Know? Unlike its close cousin the langsat, the duku's significantly thicker skin grants it a much longer shelf life after harvest and protects it from bruising easily whereby make it highly favored for long distance transport!
